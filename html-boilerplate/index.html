Feeding Birds in Winter: Why It Matters

A look at how simple garden support can make a difference

Why Winter is a Challenging Time for Birds

As the days shorten and the temperatures drop, birds face some of the toughest conditions of the year. Natural food sources become scarce when the ground freezes and insects disappear into hiding. Without easy access to seeds, berries, and insects, many species struggle to find the energy they need to survive.

The Importance of Supplmenting Bird Diets

Providing food for birds during the winter isn't just a pleasant hobby - it can be a meaningful way to help local wildlife thrive. When you put out appropriate food, you're offering birds a reliable source of calories just when they need it most. This supplemental feeding can improve survival rates and help maintain strong population numbers.

Birds burn through energy rapidly in cold weather. A few extra seeds or suet at the right time can mean the difference between life and death for a small songbird facing a frosty morning.

These plants, including perennials, trees, and shrubs, provide essential food sources and protective shelter for garden birds throughout the winter months.

During winter, particularly when snow covers the ground, non-migratory birds often struggle to locate enough food. Putting up bird feeders and suet in your garden can help species that stay behind, like cardinals and juncos. However, an even easier and more natural way to support wildlife through the colder months is to grow landscape plants that provide food and shelter for birds on their own.

Simple Ways to Support Birds in Your Garden

  • Sunflower seeds: Rich in fat and popular with many birds and species.
  • Suet or fat balls: Excellent energy sources for cold mornings.
  • Berries and fruit: Leave some on shrubs or offer bird-safe fruit in feeders.
  • Fresh water: Even in winter, birds needs water for drinking and bathing.
